# Outreach Readiness

## Overview

Use this skill to decide whether an outreach message is actually ready to send.

The goal is not to admire a draft. The goal is to judge whether it is clear, credible, appropriate, safe, and worth sending.

## Triggers

Use this skill when:

- an outreach email draft exists
- a follow-up message is being considered
- a send-ready batch is being assembled
- a pitch may go external soon
- Zee needs to decide whether more personalization or cleanup is required

## Audience And Objective Check

Before evaluating the draft itself, confirm:

- who the message is for
- what the objective is
- what the ask is
- what the recipient should understand or do next

If these are muddy, the message is not ready.

## Offer And Ask Clarity

Check whether:

- the message makes a clear point
- the offer is understandable
- the ask is reasonable and visible
- the recipient can tell why the message is relevant
- the message is short enough to read but complete enough to act on

If the ask is vague, hidden, or awkward, revise.

## Trust And Credibility Check

Review whether the message:

- sounds grounded and believable
- avoids obvious overclaiming
- avoids fake familiarity
- avoids spammy language
- avoids sounding careless, generic, or manipulative
- gives the recipient a reason to take it seriously

If the message hurts trust, it is not ready.

## Tone And Channel Fit

Check whether the tone fits:

- the recipient
- the business context
- the channel
- Dylan's standards
- Zee's professional voice

Do not send something that sounds sloppy, overly salesy, or strangely intimate.

## Privacy, Consent, And Approval Rule

Before a message is considered ready, verify:

- no sensitive data is exposed unnecessarily
- the communication is consistent with Dylan's rules
- external sending approval requirements are respected
- the message is not pretending to be someone it is not

If approval is still needed before sending, the skill may mark the draft as content-ready but not send-ready.

## Personalization Minimums

A message should usually not be treated as ready if it lacks:

- correct company identity
- correct recipient or business context
- at least one believable point of relevance
- enough tailoring to avoid reading like mass spam

The goal is not perfect personalization. The goal is enough personalization to justify the contact.

## Send / No-Send Checklist

Before calling a message ready, ask:

1. Is the audience correct?
2. Is the objective clear?
3. Is the ask clear?
4. Does the message sound credible?
5. Is the tone appropriate?
6. Is there enough personalization?
7. Does the message respect privacy and approval rules?
8. Would sending this help or hurt trust?

If the answer is weak on core points, revise.

## Rewrite Guidance

Common rewrite needs include:

- make the opening more relevant
- make the ask clearer
- remove spammy wording
- tighten length
- improve professionalism
- reduce pressure or awkwardness
- strengthen trust through specificity and clarity

## Outcomes

Return one of these:

- send-ready
- revise before send
- hold
- not appropriate to send

Include:

- what was evaluated
- the main readiness judgment
- what must change if not ready
- whether the blocker is content quality or approval status

## Guiding Principle

Use this rule:

> A message is only ready when it is clear, credible, appropriate, and aligned with the objective.

And this counter-rule:

> Do not treat a merely decent draft as send-ready if it would weaken trust or waste the contact opportunity.

## Intent

This skill exists to create a proper gate between drafting and sending.

The goal is:

- stronger outreach quality
- less careless sending
- better trust
- better use of each contact opportunity
