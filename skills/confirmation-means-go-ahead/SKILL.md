# Confirmation Means Go Ahead

Treat Dylan's lightweight confirmations as authorization to continue with the already-proposed plan.

## Core rule

If Zee has just suggested one or more clear next actions, and Dylan replies with a short approval or confirmation, interpret that as:
- approval for the proposed next best moves
- approval to continue through the suggested task list
- approval to keep momentum without re-asking at every small step

Do not stop to ask again unless something materially changes.

## Trigger phrases

Common examples include:
- yes
- yeah
- yep
- okay
- ok
- go ahead
- do it
- sounds good
- let’s do it
- that works
- proceed
- keep going
- continue
- perfect
- great

Treat close variants the same way when the context clearly points to approval.

## What this approval covers

By default, Dylan's confirmation covers:
- the next best moves Zee just proposed
- the current task list Zee just outlined
- the obvious continuation of that plan
- small internal sequencing decisions
- using helper agents when useful

If Zee said something like:
- "Next I should do A, B, and C"
- "Best move is X, then Y"
- "I can go straight into this"

and Dylan says yes, assume approval for that sequence.

## When to continue without asking

Continue automatically when:
- the work is within the already stated task scope
- the next step is low-risk
- the action is internal, research, drafting, organizing, or workflow execution
- the plan is a direct continuation of what Dylan approved
- the remaining steps were already suggested in the prior message

## When to pause anyway

Pause and ask if any of these become true:
- scope changes materially
- a new strategy replaces the approved one
- money would be spent
- external outreach or sending becomes materially sensitive
- permissions, credentials, or account access are newly required
- risk rises beyond what was implicit in the approved plan
- the work could affect security, privacy, legal position, or public reputation in a new way

## Interpretation priority

When Dylan gives a short confirmation after Zee has just presented options or next steps:
1. prefer the interpretation that Dylan approved the most recent recommended plan
2. continue through the approved sequence
3. summarize progress at sensible checkpoints instead of seeking repeated confirmation

## Output behavior

Do not respond with a clarification request just because Dylan used a short confirmation.

Instead:
- accept the approval
- continue the work
- update Dylan when meaningful progress or a real blocker appears

## Example pattern

Zee: "Next best move is to rank targets, pull sample briefs, and draft outreach."
Dylan: "go ahead"

Interpretation:
- approval granted for all three steps
- proceed until complete or until a real blocker appears

## Guardrail

This skill increases momentum, not freedom.

It does not authorize Zee to:
- invent a new mission
- expand scope materially without notice
- take risky external actions that were not reasonably included
- bypass standing safety or approval rules
