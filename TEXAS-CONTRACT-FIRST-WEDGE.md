# Texas Contract First Wedge

## Purpose
Define the narrow proving wedge that validates the eventual Texas-wide public contract platform.

## Recommended First Wedge
- **Region:** DFW
- **Category:** Janitorial / custodial / facility-cleaning related public procurement

## Why This Wedge
- enough public entities to matter
- enough fragmentation to reveal the real platform problem
- manageable compared with full-state launch
- category language is easier than highly technical procurement categories
- useful for proving normalization, filtering, and source ranking

## What This Wedge Must Prove
- we can identify relevant sources repeatedly
- we can distinguish strong sources from weak/noisy sources
- we can normalize different procurement systems into one internal format
- we can produce user-facing value from messy procurement data
- we can track open vs historical vs awarded signal clearly

## What Success Looks Like
- reliable repeatable source-monitoring workflow
- structured internal records, not ad hoc notes only
- evidence that users could search/filter/use the data
- enough confidence to expand horizontally to more categories or geographies

## What Failure Would Teach
If this wedge struggles, we would learn one or more of the following:
- the category is wrong
- the geography is wrong
- the discovery workflow is too weak
- the value is in procurement history rather than open opportunities
- the product needs different framing

## Expansion Logic
If the wedge works, expand in one of two directions:
1. same category, broader geography
2. same geography, broader categories

Recommended order:
- DFW janitorial
- wider Texas facilities-related categories
- then broader Texas public contract coverage
